Probability & Bayesian Analysis

Research Question

Does high claim amount influence customer lifetime value?

Event Definitions

  • Event A: High CLTV (> median ₹66.4K)
  • Event B: High Claim Amount (> median ₹6K)

Probability Results

Probability Value Interpretation
P(A) 0.4999 50% of customers exceed median CLTV
P(B) 0.3247 32.5% generate above-median claims
P(A ∩ B) 0.1824 18.2% have BOTH high value AND high claims
P(A|B) - CONDITIONAL 0.5613 56.1% of high-claim customers have high CLTV
P(B|A) - Reverse 0.3647 36.5% of high-CLTV customers are high-claim

🎯 Bayesian Insight: POSITIVE DEPENDENCE

Conditional Probability (0.5613) >> Marginal (0.4999)
Lift = 0.5613 / 0.4999 = 1.123 (12.3% increase)

Key Conclusion: High-claim customers are 12.3% MORE likely to have high CLTV
Industry Implication: Claims activity signals customer engagement; claims-heavy portfolios warrant retention investment, NOT auto-churn flagging

Bayes' Theorem Verification

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) × P(A)] / P(B)

P(A|B) = [0.3647 × 0.4999] / 0.3247 = 0.5613 ✓
